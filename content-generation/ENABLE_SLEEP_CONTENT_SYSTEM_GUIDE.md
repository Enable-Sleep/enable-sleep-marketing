## Pro Tips for Best Results

### 1. **One Theme Per Piece**
Don't try to cover all 5 themes in one blog post. Pick one and go deep.

### 2. **Let Dr. Zelk's Voice Shine**
Use his signature concepts, but don't force all of them into every piece. Natural integration beats keyword stuffing.

### 3. **Statistics Tell Stories**
When using stats like "80% undiagnosed," follow immediately with what that means for the dentist's practice specifically.

### 4. **Educator > Salesperson**
If a paragraph sounds like marketing copy, rewrite it as if Dr. Zelk is teaching a colleague over coffee.

### 5. **Real Examples Work Better**
Even brief, anonymized patient examples are more powerful than abstract concepts.

### 6. **End with Empowerment**
Every piece should leave dentists feeling capable and inspired, not overwhelmed.

## Questions?

Common questions and answers:

**Q: Can I adjust Dr. Zelk's voice for different platforms?**
A: Yes, but maintain core characteristics. LinkedIn can be more professional, emails more conversational, but always educator-first.

**Q: Should every piece mention Enable Sleep the platform?**
A: No. Many awareness-stage pieces should focus on the problem and Dr. Zelk's expertise. Product comes later.

**Q: How much of Beautiful Faces content can I reference?**
A: Liberally reference concepts and insights, but don't republish long passages. The book establishes credibility; your content applies it to dentists' needs.

**Q: What if I need content on topics not in the 5 themes?**
A: The 5 themes are starting points. You can create briefs for other topics, but ensure they tie back to one of the core themes and follow the voice guidelines.

**Q: Can I use these for video scripts / podcast outlines?**
A: Absolutely. The voice profile works for any medium. Adjust length and pacing for spoken word, but maintain the core voice.
